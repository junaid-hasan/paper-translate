<html><body><h1 data-bbox="257 57 787 91">Rationale Singularities of Complex Surfaces</h1><p data-bbox="59 115 939 277">Let $R$ be the local ring of a 2-dimensional normal singularity $(X,x)$, further let $f: X' \to X$ be a resolution of the singularities, $C = f^{-1}(x)$, and $C_1, \dots, C_k$ the irreducible components of $C$. The resolution is chosen so that the $C_i$ are singularity-free and intersect transversally. From the usual short exact sequence</p><div class="formula" data-bbox="357 290 639 350">$0 \to \mathbb{Z} \to \mathcal{O}_{X'} \to \mathcal{O}_{X'}^* \to 0$</div><p data-bbox="59 365 939 450">and the corresponding long exact sequence of direct images, Mumford deduces an exact sequence</p><div class="formula" data-bbox="59 465 880 525">(1) $0 \to H^1(C,\mathbb{Z}) \to (R^1f_*\mathcal{O}_{X'})_x \to (R^1f_*\mathcal{O}_{X'}^*)_x \to H^2(C,\mathbb{Z}) \to 0$.</div><p data-bbox="59 540 639 575">Furthermore, there exists a canonical surjection.</p><div class="formula" data-bbox="387 585 707 645">(2) $(R^1f_*\mathcal{O}_{X'}^*)_x \to C(\mathcal{O}_{X,x}) \to 0$,</div><p data-bbox="59 655 939 840">whose kernel consists of the group of cycles $\sum n_i C_i$. Let $M$ be the boundary of a "good" neighborhood of $x$. (Such a neighborhood can be constructed as in Mumford or via a local embedding of $(X,x)$ as the intersection of $X$ with a small sphere around $x$.) Let $H_1(M,\mathbb{Z})_0$ be the torsion subgroup of $H_1(M,\mathbb{Z})$. Then Mumford obtains from (1) and (2) an exact sequence.</p><div class="formula" data-bbox="59 855 880 915">(3) $0 \to H^1(C,\mathbb{Z}) \to (R^1f_*\mathcal{O}_{X'})_x \to C(\mathcal{O}_{X,x}) \to H_1(M,\mathbb{Z})_0 \to 0$.</div><p data-bbox="59 930 939 965">From (3) and 1.4 (ii) one easily deduces (cf. Storch [29] §6, Satz 1).</p></body></html>
